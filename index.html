The VICARIOS Mixed Reality and Simulations Lab aims to advance the understanding of human interaction with remote environments, real or virtual. Through approaches in immersive mixed reality, telerobotics, and biomechanical simulations and control, we stimulate a human user to vicariously experience the remote environment.

Our research stands on three main pillars:

To enhance real-time immersive remote perception
Presence and Immersion are the cornerstone of intuitive interaction in remote telepresence applications. It requires high-fidelity in control actions as well as perception (vision, haptics, auditory, and other sensory feedback) of the remote environment. We are studying how the combination of immersive mixed reality (MR) interfaces, intuitive control devices, real-time data from remote sensors (RGB-D cameras, microphones, F/T sensors, etc.) can allow real-time immersive remote interaction for the human user.

To advance state-of-the-art in immersive training through eXtended Reality (xR)
serves as a promising technology demonstration for safety training, providing risk free, immersive learning, among other features. However, training especially in dangerous occupations, e.g., working-at-heights, can be considered effective only if successfully transferable to the work environment, and lasting in time. We are exploring how xR technologies, combining VR with spatially contextualized physical interaction can help make training sessions more effective than conventional methods when it comes to the acquisition of safety behaviour, while increasing the trainee’s engagement.

To understand human interaction behaviour with a focus on human factors
The overarching objective of our research is to improve our knowledge of how humans interact with remote environments through the medium of xR. Human factors and ergonomics, both physical and cognitive, play a vital role in the acceptance and use of xR. We are investigating how predictive full-body biomechanical simulations and biophysiological parameter tracking in the real world can help understand a human user’s behaviour during their interaction with remote environments.

Funding

ROBOT TELEOPERATIVO 2

This project aims at enhanced occupational safety in hazardous environments through substitution, i.e., removing the worker from the unsafe area and having robotic technologies do the same tasks through remote robotic teleoperation. To achieve this goal, a collaborative robotic teleoperation system is proposed.

More details here: https://advr.iit.it/projects/inail-scc/teleoperazione

Duration: 1-Jan-2021 -to- 31-Dec-2023

CADUTA dall’ALTO (Falling from Heights)

The project is aimed at the design and development of novel strategies and solutions aimed at preventing accidents as well as protecting workers working at heights. The project will focus on advancements in wearable sensing & actuation technologies, including technologies for fall impact reduction and smart monitoring, as well as on new paradigms in immersive training for workers.

Duration: 1-Jan-2021 -to- 31-Dec-2023
